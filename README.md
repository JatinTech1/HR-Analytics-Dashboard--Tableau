# HR-Analytics-Dashboard--Tableau
This project is an HR Analytics Dashboard designed to analyze employee data and identify key trends related to attrition, demographics, job satisfaction, and workforce distribution. The dashboard helps HR teams and management make data-driven decisions to improve employee retention and workplace satisfaction.
This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition patterns within an organization and presenting the insights through an interactive HR Analytics Dashboard.

## The main objective was to help HR teams and business leaders understand:

Why employees are leaving the company

Which departments are most affected

How age, gender, and job role impact attrition

How job satisfaction connects with employee turnover

The dashboard transforms raw HR data into clear, actionable insights that support better workforce planning and retention strategies.

## Objectives

Identify departments with the highest attrition

Analyze attrition by age group and gender

Study attrition by education field and job role

Measure overall workforce health

Highlight dissatisfaction trends among employees

## Key Insights
## Department-wise Attrition

Sales Department – 56.12% (Highest attrition)

R&D – 38.82%

HR – 5.06% (Lowest attrition)

 Insight: Sales team requires immediate attention with better incentives and engagement programs.

## Education Field-wise Attrition
Field	Attrition Count
Life Sciences	89
Medical	63
Marketing	35
Technical Degree	32
Other	11
Human Resources	7

 Insight: Employees with technical and medical backgrounds tend to switch jobs more frequently.

## Attrition by Age Group & Gender

Highest attrition in 25–34 age group

Males have higher attrition than females

Young professionals (<35 years) show more turnover

 Insight: Younger employees are more likely to explore better career and salary opportunities.

## Job Satisfaction Analysis

Roles with lower job satisfaction:

Sales Executives

Laboratory Technicians

Research Scientists

Roles with higher satisfaction:

Managers

Directors

 Insight: Entry-level and technical positions need improvement in work-life balance and growth opportunities.
